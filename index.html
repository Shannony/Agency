Thoughts on the 2016 election.

moving forward

Similar to when Blair Waldorf found out that Nate had cheated on her with Serena, I was shocked last Tuesday. But in my case, Nate was the American public and Serena was instead a billionaire, presidential candidate Donald Trump.

As you've probably guessed, I was in the Hillary camp. Just check out this blurry selfie I took.

The manic look in my eyes pretty much confirms the fact that I would have body tackled anyone that stood in the way of me and Hillary.I had to wait 6 hours in the cold, but it was so worth it. I made friends with Secret Service Agent Nick (pictured here with his gold pin blocking me from potentially tackling the former Secretary of State) and I got to speak to members of the press and observe how they cover candidates on the campaign trail. Of course, the best part of the night was when Hillary walked out with Secret Service flanking her sides, rocking an iconic blue pantsuit, and pumping her fist to the cheers of “Hillary! Hillary! Hillary!”

First impressions:

1) She’s gorgeous. Who knew someone could rock a pantsuit like that?!
2) She’s not as “robotic” as the media portrays her to be. She made sure to shake hands with all her supporters, thanking them for coming out to cheer her on. She is also great at connecting with people, making eye contact so that they know she’s actually listening to their concerns.
3) Soft hands. When she reached me, I shook her hand and asked her to keep supporting young women in STEM. She thanked me and responded with promises of more action in the future... but I honestly couldn't focus cause of how soft her hands were. Seriously, what kind of lotion do professional politicians use, because I felt like I was touching the skin of a professional hand model.

If these may seem like superficial, surface observations, you’re absolutely right. But in all honesty, that’s all I could gather from the 2 minutes I met her.

These two minutes are not why I became a Hillary supporter. To a 17 year old girl who prides herself in hard work and big dreams, a Hillary win would have shown that a woman with 30 years of political experience and public service could finally hold the highest office in America. It would have represented a rejection of words of sexual harassment, islamophobia, and paranoia. It would send the message that the glass ceiling is close to cracking, and that someone like me could one day become president too. When she lost it felt like a slap in the face.


WHY I’M OPTIMISTIC ABOUT THE FUTURE

We all know how this story ends. Trump wins.

As a supporter of the losing party who disagreed with many of the things said on the campaign trail, why would I possibly be optimistic about the future?

Because I believe that these election results have shown people like me, a millennial living in the Bay Area, that not everyone shares similar beliefs and points of view as me.

This election has fired me up for the future. It’s revitalized the “fighting spirit” in me. There are people out there that believe that women are incapable or inferior. Let’s prove them wrong! I’m committed now more than ever to fight to close the gender wage gap especially through bringing more women into computer science, bringing more political power to minorities especially to Asian Americans, and proving people wrong by example. It’s shown me that sexism and racism are very much alive. It has shown me that the only way fight that is to have your voice heard and to speak up for what you believe in. I don’t know what the next four years will be like, but I do know that the result of this election have motivated my generation to become more passionate and politically active citizens.

Afterall, what’s the joy in winning, if there aren’t a few losses on the road to victory?

“I hope you make the best of it. I hope you see things that startle you. I hope you feel things you never felt before. I hope you meet people who have a different point of view. I hope you lead a life you’re proud of, and if you’re not, I hope you have the courage to start all over again” - F. Scott Fitzgerald
